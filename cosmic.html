COSMIC Lunar CubeSat Mission

A High-Fidelity Lunar Orbiting Mission Designed by a 21-Person Team

Spring 2024 21-Person Team Princeton University MAE 342, Space System Design

Mission

  • Design a high-fidelity lunar orbiting mission to provide tracking and mission support in the cislunar regime
  • 21-person team divided into subsystem pairs — I was responsible for the Electrical and Power subsystem and the Launch Vehicle subsystem
  • Final Design Report presented to NASA scientists at Goddard Space Center, with critical feedback received and implemented for the final design iteration

Mission Architecture

  • The Cislunar Object Surveying and Mission Infrastructure Constellation (COSMIC) consists of 7 18U CubeSats placed in L1 Halo, frozen lunar, and L2 Halo orbits
  • Provides tracking coverage of Low Lunar Orbit (LLO), Near Rectilinear Halo Orbit (NRHO), L1 Halo, L2 Halo, and low thrust transfers from GTO to L1/L2 Halo

Electrical & Power Subsystem

  • Aided in the design of the power budget for the CubeSat constellation
  • Selected the correct battery for the CubeSats based on mission requirements and orbital constraints

Launch Vehicle Subsystem

  • Selected the correct launch vehicle for the mission
  • Ensured the vehicle met the mission requirements for Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC)

Teamwork & Communication

  • With 21 students, communication was absolutely essential — a Slack workspace was used and a team lead coordinated every channel to ensure the correct subsystems were communicating
  • Learned to expand breadth of work to communicate with as many relevant subsystems as possible, allowing them to work more effectively as well
  • A great exercise in project management on the individual level

Results

  • Presented the Final Design Report to NASA scientists at Goddard Space Center
  • Critical feedback was received and implemented for the final design iteration
  • A transformative experience showcasing how real engineers receive feedback on high-fidelity missions
