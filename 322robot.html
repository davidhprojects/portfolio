Autonomous Search & Rescue Robot

A 10-Person Collaborative Project to Design and Build an Autonomous Obstacle-Navigating Robot

Fall 2024 10-Person Team Princeton University MAE 322

Mission

  • Design and build an autonomous robot to traverse an obstacle course, navigate toward a light source, and deliver a medical kit
  • 10-person collaborative team — I was primarily responsible for manufacturing and testing/validation of the autonomous code
  • Secondary goal: complete the course as fast as possible
  • The team went with a "Perry The Platypus" theme and 3D printed a cover for the robot

Constraints

  • 8-foot ramp traversal
  • Dogleg chute: three 6–8 foot segments, 18" plywood walls, 3-foot spacing
  • 1-foot high wall with a 6" step
  • Must survive a 1-foot drop test

Design Overview

  • Chassis: Three-wheeled vehicle with aluminum frame mounted on a laser-cut acrylic base plate
  • Drivetrain: DC CIM motor driving rear wheels via chain-and-sprocket; servo-controlled front wheel for steering
  • Power: 12V battery
  • Traction: 3D printed rubber hooks on wheels
  • Med Kit Delivery: Flexible rubber wheel grabs cube-shaped med kit and spins to throw it into the box
  • Controller: Arduino Teensy microcontroller running a state-machine-based control algorithm
  • Sensors: Infrared sensors (wall detection), photoresistor array (light-source tracking), bump sensors (obstacle detection)

Results

  • Robot successfully traversed all obstacles autonomously
  • Significant machine shop hours required — strong team communication was essential
  • An incredible teambuilding exercise across design, manufacturing, and testing
