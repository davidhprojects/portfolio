Autonomous Search & Rescue Robot

A 10-Person Collaborative Project to Design and Build an Autonomous Obstacle-Navigating Robot

Spring 2025 10-Person Team Princeton University

Abstract

The objective of this project was to design a Search and Rescue Robot that can autonomously traverse an obstacle course, navigate toward a light source, and deliver a medical kit. The course includes an 8-foot ramp, a dogleg chute comprised of three 6-8 foot segments with 18" plywood walls, and a 1-foot high wall with a 6" step. A secondary goal was to complete the course as fast as possible. The robot also had to survive a 1-foot drop test.

The robot was designed as a three-wheeled vehicle with an aluminum chassis. The wheels feature 3D printed rubber hooks for better traction, and the robot is powered by a 12V battery using a DC CIM motor for propulsion. An Arduino Teensy microcontroller controls the motors and sensors and runs the autonomous navigation script.

This project was completed in a group of 10 students and proved to be an incredible teambuilding exercise. I was primarily responsible for the manufacturing of the robot, as well as the testing and validation of the autonomous code. Because it involved many machine shop hours, communication was essential in completing the project efficiently. Ultimately, the team chose to go with a "Perry The Platypus" theme and 3D printed a cover for the robot!

Design Overview

The robot uses a carefully designed aluminum frame mounted on a laser-cut acrylic base plate. A single DC CIM motor drives the rear wheels through a chain-and-sprocket system, while a servo-controlled front wheel handles steering. The medical kit delivery system uses a flexible rubber wheel that grabs the cube-shaped med kit and spins on command to deliver it.

The autonomous navigation relies on infrared sensors for wall detection in the dogleg chute, a photoresistor array for light-source tracking, and bump sensors for obstacle detection. All sensor data is processed by the Teensy microcontroller running a state-machine-based control algorithm.
