1954 Triumph Terrier Restoration

A Complete Restoration of a Vintage Motorcycle in a Hands-On Freshman Seminar

Spring 2022 17-Person Team Princeton University FRS 106

Mission

  • Complete a full restoration of a 1954 Triumph Terrier motorcycle as part of Princeton's freshman seminar FRS 106: The Art & Science of Motorcycle Design
  • 17-person team divided into six subsystem groups — I was responsible for the Top End (cylinder head, valves, and rocker assemblies)
  • Hands-on engineering from day one: disassembly, sandblasting, machining, painting, and final assembly over the course of one semester

Top End — My Subsystem

  • Sandblasted and cleaned the cylinder head, then lapped it to create a flat mating surface for proper sealing
  • Used bluing compound to verify proper valve seating and lapped both valves using coarse and fine grinding compounds
  • Installed dual valve springs — the inner spring oriented opposite to prevent torsion and maintain straight valve compression
  • Installed both rocker assemblies, reconnected oil lines, and fitted new valve cover gaskets
  • Located and applied black high-temperature paint for the cylinder barrel
  • Tested a previous year's restored motorcycle with Professor Littman, learning to reset timing at top-dead center and tune the carburetor

Other Subsystems

  • Bottom End: Disassembled the engine internals, replaced worn bearings and seals, pressed components at the MAE machine shop, and reassembled the flywheel, chain, and clutch basket
  • Frame & Forks: Sandblasted, primed, and painted the frame red; sanded and rebuilt the front fork assembly with greased ball bearings and bronze bushings
  • Carburetor & Electrical: Disassembled and ultrasonically cleaned the carburetor; rebuilt the ignition circuit by resoldering stator wires and fabricated a custom ignition coil bracket
  • Wheels & Brakes: Sandblasted and painted hubs, relaced wheels with new spokes under expert guidance, trued wheels on a truing stand for lateral and radial accuracy
  • Detailing, Tanks & Cables: Used an English wheel to remove fender dents, applied body filler and multi-stage automotive paint, installed fuel tank badges, and designed custom team apparel

Skills & Takeaways

  • Gained hands-on experience with machine shop tools — sandblasters, lathes, taps and dies, English wheel, and truing stands
  • Learned the fundamentals of single-cylinder engine mechanics: valve timing, compression sealing, carburetor tuning, and ignition circuits
  • Collaborated across subsystem groups to ensure components interfaced correctly during final assembly
  • Presented the completed restoration to the Princeton community in May 2022
